## EMAIL 3 — Day 4
**Subject:** The canning mistake that could harm your family (and how to avoid it)

If you are new to home canning, there is one rule that cannot be broken:

Water bath canning is ONLY for high-acid foods.

Green beans, corn, carrots, meats, soups — ALL of these require pressure canning. Using water bath for low-acid foods creates conditions for botulism — a potentially fatal foodborne illness with no smell and no taste.

This is not meant to scare you. It is meant to protect you. Canning is completely safe when done correctly.

Read our complete canning safety guide → [LINK]/tools (click Canning Guide tab)

It covers both methods step by step with safety at the center.

The homesteader who preserves safely feeds their family well — not just today, but all year long.
