## EMAIL 3 — Day 4
**Subject:** Is my pet suffering? The honest question no one wants to ask.

If you are caring for a senior or seriously ill pet, you may be carrying one of the heaviest questions a pet owner faces:

Is my pet still having good days?

We built the Quality of Life Assessment tool to help you organize your observations — not to replace your veterinarian, but to help you prepare for that conversation.

Rate 7 areas (hurt, hunger, hydration, hygiene, happiness, mobility, more good days than bad) on a scale of 1-10.

A total above 35 generally suggests acceptable quality of life.
Below 35 warrants a serious conversation with your vet.

Try it here → [LINK]/tools — Quality of Life tab

This tool was developed from the publicly available HHHHHMM scale by Dr. Alice Villalobos. Always consult your veterinarian for any end-of-life decisions.

Your vet is your most important partner right now. This tool helps you show up to that appointment prepared.
